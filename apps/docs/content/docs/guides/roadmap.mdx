## Overview

The **Roadmap** page gives you a visual overview of what's planned and in progress. It helps you prioritize work and communicate plans to your team and users.

## Roadmap Columns

The roadmap is organized into columns:

- **In Progress**: Ideas currently being worked on
- **Planned**: Ideas scheduled for future work
- **Completed**: Ideas that have been finished

## Using the Roadmap

### Viewing Ideas

- Each idea appears as a card showing:
  - Title and description
  - Reaction count
  - Status indicator
- Click any card to see full details

### Updating Status

- **Drag and drop**: Move ideas between columns to update their status
- **Click to edit**: Open idea details to make changes
- **Bulk updates**: Select multiple ideas to update at once

### Prioritization

- **Reaction counts**: See which ideas users want most
- **Visual organization**: Group related ideas together
- **Status tracking**: Keep statuses current to reflect reality

## Best Practices

### Keeping It Updated

- **Regular reviews**: Update the roadmap regularly
- **Accurate statuses**: Keep statuses current
- **Move completed items**: Show progress by moving completed ideas

### Communication

- **Share with team**: Use the roadmap to align your team
- **Public visibility**: Consider making your roadmap public
- **Set expectations**: Use the roadmap to communicate timelines

### Organization

- **Group related ideas**: Organize by feature area or product line
- **Use boards**: Create boards to categorize ideas
- **Filter views**: Use filters to focus on specific areas

## Tips & Tricks

1. **Quick updates**: Drag and drop is the fastest way to update statuses
2. **Visual planning**: Use the roadmap view for planning sessions
3. **User feedback**: Share the roadmap to get user input on priorities
4. **Progress tracking**: Regularly review to track progress against goals
